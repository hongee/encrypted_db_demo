A Smart Nation faces Smarter Security Challenges

See how Singtel steps up to tackle the biggest security challenges of today's smarter cities and bigger data.

Privacy

You wouldn't want your personal data in the hands of the wrong people, and neither would your customers. Recent high profile database breaches in organisations such as Sony and Ashley Madison have underscored the need for better privacy and data protection measures.

Computation

Data is no longer static. Business decisions are increasingly data driven operations, and Smart Nation initiatives will require frequent queries and analytics on large datasets. With the database always "live", at-rest encryption is no longer a viable technique.

Decentralized Data

The scale and requirements of Big Data has made it unfeasible to operate Datacenters in house. While shifting data and computation to the cloud offers significant savings through economies of scale, that data is now also managed by an untrusted third party.

Why Anonymization isn't Enough

Current Privacy Protection Schemes

Sensitive information (i.e. Names, ID Numbers, Taxi License/Medallion Information) is stored as a hash using a deterministic one way function (such as MD5) to ensure the anonymity of information. This helps to protect the identities of individuals in the case of a database breach or, in this case, when the data is openly shared.

Vulnerabilities

Such security measures, however, do not provide semantic security. They are vulnerable to a wide range of attacks (such as Rainbow Tables) that make it possible to retrieve plaintext data by precomputing the hashes for a large number of license numbers, and then doing a simple look up on the table.

Demand for Information Security

New Smart Nation initiatives will require even more data with even greater granularity. With the increased use of personal identifying data, compromises in data security will no longer just be threats to your business, but also to the security and privacy of your partners, customers and perhaps even yourself.

Full Database Encryption

SQL-Aware Encryption

By encrypting entire databases with algorithms specifically tailored to the types of queries that will be performed, we can compute and query over encrypted data and minimize the exposure of plaintext information.

Guarantees Against Untrusted Providers

The encrypted database is designed such that only encrypted data and queries are stored, transmitted and executed on untrusted third party domains, guaranteeing the integrity of you and your customers' valuable data.

Minimum Performance Penalties

The database employs dynamic encryption strategies that continuously adjust the level of encryption to suit your needs while ensuring the highest possible levels of data security.

How this works

A quick overview of the fully encrypted database system.

Application Server

The application server simply sends regular SQL queries to a SQL proxy instead of querying the database directly. The encrypted database can hence essentially be a drop in replacement for most use cases.

SQL Proxy

The SQL proxy securely manages the encryption keys and schemes and translates the plaintext SQL queries to the appropriate encrypted form. All data and queries, including table and column names, are encrypted beyond this point.

Untrusted Domain

Despite the transition to the public cloud, the queries and data remain safely encrypted. This can be employed in junction with common security measures such as SSL, to ensure that the data in-transit is well protected against snooping and man-in-the-middle attacks.

Offsite Database

The encrypted queries are executed directly over the database's encrypted data, without revealing any plaintext information. This protects against malicious external database administrators, as well as any potential breaches of the external database.
